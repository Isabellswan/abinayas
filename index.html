Welcome, Visitor

I am an Electrical and Electronics Engineering (Sandwich) graduate from PSG College of Technology, currently working in the IT industry while preparing to transition into advanced study in space science and theoretical physics. My long-term ambition is to contribute to humanity’s exploration and understanding of the universe through both scientific research and the development of technologies that enable space exploration. My academic interests focus on astrophysics, cosmology, gravitational physics, and the fundamental laws governing the universe. I am particularly interested in questions concerning the structure and evolution of the cosmos, the nature of gravity, and the physical processes that shape astronomical systems. To pursue these interests, I am actively strengthening my foundations in theoretical physics and mathematical methods while exploring computational approaches to modelling physical phenomena. Alongside scientific inquiry, I maintain a strong interest in the technological challenges that make space exploration possible, including spacecraft systems, planetary exploration technologies, satellite systems, and space telescopes. I believe that meaningful progress in space exploration emerges from the intersection of rigorous scientific understanding and innovative engineering design. Through continuous study, research, and collaboration with the scientific community, I aim to pursue graduate studies in theoretical physics and space science and contribute to advancing humanity’s knowledge of the cosmos.
